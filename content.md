## About Section

**Label:** Who we are
**Title:** Made with love

Ale Skate Club is a **non-professional, community-driven skateboard crew** based in Warsaw. We started this club because we believe skating is for everyone — regardless of age, skill, or whether you even own a board.

Our name says it all: **"Ale"** in Polish means "But" — as in, *"But wait — you CAN skate."* We break down every barrier to entry and replace it with stoke, community, and solid sessions.

We gather three times a week at spots across Warsaw. Sessions are casual, supportive, and always a good time. Whether you want to land your first ollie or just cruise around with friendly people, you belong here.

## Schedule Section

**Label:** When we skate
**Title:** Regular Meetings

### Tuesday — Skateboard Tuesday (flagship session)

Our main weekly event. This is the core of Ale Skate Club — all levels welcome, from complete beginners to more experienced riders. We pick a spot, we skate, we hang. **Bring:** yourself. Everything else we can sort out.

### Thursday — Mid-week skate session

The mid-week session to keep the momentum going. Great for working on what you tried on Tuesday, meeting new people, and exploring different spots around the city. **Vibe:** chill and exploratory.

### Saturday — Weekend session

The weekend session draws the biggest crowd. More time, more energy, more spots. Perfect for those who can't make it during the week. Sometimes we organize longer skate trips across Warsaw. **Duration:** as long as we want.

### 📍 How to find us

Meeting locations are announced on our **Instagram @aleskate.club** before each session. Follow us to stay updated — we sometimes go to different spots depending on weather, season, and what feels good that week.

## Spots Section

**Label:** Where we skate
**Title:** Warsaw Spots

Warsaw has a surprisingly rich skate scene. From iconic free outdoor skateparks to underground DIY gems, the city has something for every style. Here are some of the spots on our regular rotation.

### Spot 01 — AvePark (indoor)

Warsaw's largest indoor skatepark — 3,000 sqm of street, park, and bowl sections. Heated in winter. Perfect for year-round sessions when the weather turns. Equipment rental on-site for beginners.

**Tags:** indoor · all levels · gear rental

### Spot 02 — Woodpark (indoor)

Created by skaters, for skaters. Woodpark features a wooden volatile section plus a full concrete street section with the longest handrail in Poland, funboxes, and more. Beginner-friendly schools operate here too.

**Tags:** indoor · street · bowl

### Spot 03 — Świętokrzyski Bridge Skatepark (street)

A compact, cleverly designed skatepark sheltered under the bridge — rain or shine, day or night. Funboxes, grindboxes, and fun obstacles. Lit after dark, so evening sessions are very much on the table.

**Tags:** outdoor · sheltered · night sessions

### Spot 04 — Pole Mokotowskie (plaza)

Central Warsaw park with a large concrete plaza featuring stairs, rails, and ledges. A classic gathering spot for the Warsaw skate community — people-watching is half the fun here.

**Tags:** free · central · outdoor

### Spot 05 — Shaber Bowl (diy)

Warsaw's legendary DIY spot — born from a group of skate enthusiasts who literally moved 100 tons of rubble with their hands. 2,000 sqm of pure soul. A stolen bowl, a place with history. One of a kind in Poland.

**Tags:** diy · iconic · bowl

### Spot 06 — Służewiec Skatepark (plaza)

One of the most popular outdoor skateparks in Warsaw — large concrete bowl with extensive street obstacles including stairs and rails. Free, open year-round, and always busy with the local scene.

**Tags:** free · concrete · all year

## FAQ Section

**Label:** Questions
**Title:** FAQ

### Do I need my own skateboard to join?

Absolutely not. If you don't have a board, no worries — we'll figure it out together. Club members often share boards, especially with beginners. For indoor sessions at spots like AvePark, there's also gear rental available on-site.

### I've never skated before. Is this club for me?

100% yes. Ale Skate Club is explicitly non-professional and welcomes complete beginners with open arms. Our community is built on the idea that everyone starts somewhere. You'll find patient, encouraging people who remember exactly what it was like to stand on a board for the first time.

### Is there a membership fee?

No. Ale Skate Club is a community club — free to join and free to participate in outdoor sessions. For indoor park sessions (AvePark, Woodpark), you'd pay that park's entry fee directly, but there's no club fee on top of that.

### Where exactly are sessions held?

Session locations vary — that's part of the fun! We explore different spots across Warsaw, from the Świętokrzyski bridge skatepark and Pole Mokotowskie to indoor parks in winter. The exact spot for each session is announced on our Instagram (@aleskate.club) beforehand, so follow us to stay updated.

### What should I wear / bring?

Comfortable clothes you can move in. Closed-toe shoes with flat soles are best (think Vans, Converse, or any skate shoe). Knee pads and wrist guards are recommended for beginners — safety first. Bring water and good energy. That's it.

### Are sessions still on in winter?

Yes! Warsaw winters don't stop us. We move sessions indoors to spots like AvePark (heated, 3000 sqm, Warsaw's largest indoor skatepark) and Woodpark during colder months. The Świętokrzyski Bridge spot is also sheltered and usable year-round.

### Can kids join?

Skateboarding has no age limit. Younger skaters are welcome — just make sure parents are aware of the meetup location and timing. Indoor parks like AvePark even have dedicated kids sessions and beginner-friendly areas with foam pits.
