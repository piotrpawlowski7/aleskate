Who we are

Made with
love

Ale Skate Club is a non-professional, community-driven skateboard crew based in Warsaw. We started this club because we believe skating is for everyone — regardless of age, skill, or whether you even own a board.

Our name says it all: "Ale" in Polish means "But" — as in, "But wait — you CAN skate." We break down every barrier to entry and replace it with stoke, community, and solid sessions.

We gather three times a week at spots across Warsaw. Sessions are casual, supportive, and always a good time. Whether you want to land your first ollie or just cruise around with friendly people, you belong here.

Where we skate

Warsaw
Spots

Warsaw has a surprisingly rich skate scene. From iconic free outdoor skateparks to underground DIY gems, the city has something for every style. Here are some of the spots on our regular rotation.

01 / indoor AvePark

Warsaw's largest indoor skatepark — 3,000 sqm of street, park, and bowl sections. Heated in winter. Perfect for year-round sessions when the weather turns. Equipment rental on-site for beginners.

indoor · all levels · gear rental
02 / indoor Woodpark

Created by skaters, for skaters. Woodpark features a wooden volatile section plus a full concrete street section with the longest handrail in Poland, funboxes, and more. Beginner-friendly schools operate here too.

indoor · street · bowl
03 / street Świętokrzyski Bridge Skatepark

A compact, cleverly designed skatepark sheltered under the bridge — rain or shine, day or night. Funboxes, grindboxes, and fun obstacles. Lit after dark, so evening sessions are very much on the table.

outdoor · sheltered · night sessions
04 / plaza Pole Mokotowskie

Central Warsaw park with a large concrete plaza featuring stairs, rails, and ledges. A classic gathering spot for the Warsaw skate community — people-watching is half the fun here.

free · central · outdoor
05 / diy Shaber Bowl

Warsaw's legendary DIY spot — born from a group of skate enthusiasts who literally moved 100 tons of rubble with their hands. 2,000 sqm of pure soul. A stolen bowl, a place with history. One of a kind in Poland.

diy · iconic · bowl
06 / plaza Służewiec Skatepark

One of the most popular outdoor skateparks in Warsaw — large concrete bowl with extensive street obstacles including stairs and rails. Free, open year-round, and always busy with the local scene.

free · concrete · all year
